# MyAlarm Security - Google Ranking Improvement Plan

A plain-English guide to getting more customers finding you on Google.

---

**The good news:** MyAlarm Security already shows up on page 1 of Google for
several searches like "burglar alarm installer Bexley Sidcup". The site has

family-business story. That's a strong foundation.

**The challenge:** Several competitors have advantages that Google cares about.

| Competitor | What they have that MyAlarm doesn't |
|---|---|

## What people are actually searching for (and what Google wants to show them)

When someone in Bexley or Bromley types into Google, they're usually searching
for one of these things:

1. **"burglar alarm installer near me"** / **"CCTV installer [town name]"**
   - They want a local, trustworthy company to come and fit a system
   - Google shows the Map Pack (the map with 3 businesses) first, then websites
   - To win here: strong Google Business Profile + local content + reviews

2. **"how much does a burglar alarm cost?"**
   - They're researching before they buy
   - Google shows guides with price ranges and breakdowns
   - MyAlarm has NO content answering this question (big missed opportunity)

3. **"best burglar alarm system UK"** / **"wired vs wireless alarm"**
   - They're comparing options and want expert advice
   - Google shows helpful guides from people who clearly know their stuff
   - This is where E-E-A-T matters most (more on this below)

4. **"burglar alarm service near me"** / **"alarm battery replacement"**
   - They already have a system and need help with it
   - This is a great entry point - help them now and they may buy later
   - MyAlarm mentions servicing but doesn't have dedicated helpful content

## What is E-E-A-T and why does it matter?

E-E-A-T stands for **Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness**.
It's how Google judges whether your website deserves to rank highly. Think of
it as Google asking: "Should I trust this website enough to recommend it to my
users?"

Here's how MyAlarm currently scores, and what to improve:

### Experience (do you show real, hands-on experience?)

**Current score: Medium**

- The about page mentions 27+ years experience - good
- There's a photo of Matt and his daughter - good, it's personal
- BUT there are no photos of actual installations or work being done
- No "day in the life" content, no before/after photos of jobs
- No video content showing real expertise

**What to do:**
- Take photos of every job (with customer permission) - the van parked
  outside, the neat cabling, the finished panel on the wall, the tidy camera
  install
- Add these to blog posts like "Burglar alarm installation in [town] -
  here's what we did"
- Even a short phone video of Matt explaining something about alarms would be
  powerful

### Expertise (do you actually know what you're talking about?)

**Current score: Medium**

- The content is good and clearly written by someone who knows alarms
- BUT it doesn't mention specific qualifications or training
- Doesn't mention specific brands installed (Texecom? HikVision? Pyronix?)
- No educational content helping people understand their options
- Competitors like Spy Alarms specifically name their NSI Gold certification
  on every page

**What to do:**
- List Matt's qualifications and any training courses completed
- Name the brands you install and are trained on - people search for
  "Texecom installer near me" and "HikVision CCTV installer Kent"
- Write helpful blog posts answering common questions (see content ideas
  below)

### Authoritativeness (do other people recognise you as an authority?)

**Current score: Medium-Low**

- 122 five-star reviews is excellent... but only 26 are on Google
- No NSI or SSAIB accreditation (this is the single biggest gap)
- Not listed on key industry directories
- No mentions in local press or community groups

**What to do:**
- The #1 priority is to look into getting NSI or SSAIB accreditation (see
  Priority 1 below)
- Ask happy customers to leave a Google review (not just Checkatrade)
- Get listed on local business directories with consistent name, address,
  phone number

### Trustworthiness (can people trust you?)

**Current score: Good**

- DBS checked and insured - mentioned on the site
- Real name, real address, real phone number
- Clear pricing with no hidden costs
- Family business with personal story

**What to do:**
- Add the DBS/insurance info more prominently - maybe a dedicated "Why trust
  us" section
- If you get accredited, display the badges prominently
- Add more specific customer stories with permission

## The improvement plan (in order of priority)

### Priority 1: Industry accreditation (THE biggest win)

**What:** Look into getting NSI or SSAIB accreditation.

**Why this matters so much:**
- Google sees accredited companies as more authoritative and trustworthy
- Customers' insurance companies often require an accredited installer -
  right now, a customer choosing MyAlarm might not get an insurance discount,
  but a customer choosing Spy Alarms (NSI Gold) would
- The police will only respond to monitored alarms installed by an accredited
  company
- It's the single thing that most separates MyAlarm from the top competitors

**What the owner needs to do:**
- Visit https://www.nsi.org.uk or https://www.ssaib.org to understand what's
  involved
- It typically means being audited on your work quality, record-keeping, and
  staff training
- Once accredited, add the badge to every page of the website and mention it
  in all marketing

**On the website:**
- Once accredited, the CMS can be used to add the accreditation logo and
  text to the About page and homepage
- Mention it in the meta descriptions too

### Priority 3: Boost Google reviews (free, just takes asking)

**What:** MyAlarm has 122 reviews across all platforms but only 26 on Google.
Google reviews are the ones that matter most for ranking in the Map Pack
(the map with 3 businesses that appears at the top of local searches).

**Why it matters:**
- More Google reviews = higher position in the Map Pack
- The Map Pack gets more clicks than any other search result
- Spy Alarms has 64 reviews on Yell alone

**What the owner needs to do:**
- After every job, ask the customer: "Would you mind leaving us a quick
  Google review? It really helps us as a small business."
- Send them the Google review link (you already have one set up)
- Maybe print a small card with a QR code linking to the Google review page
  to hand to customers after a job
- Don't offer incentives for reviews (against Google's rules) - just ask
  nicely
- Aim for 50+ Google reviews as a first target

### Priority 4: Write helpful content that answers real questions

**What:** Create blog posts that answer the questions people are actually
typing into Google. This brings people to your website who are thinking about
getting an alarm or CCTV, and positions you as the expert.

**Why it matters:**
- People searching "how much does a burglar alarm cost" are potential
  customers doing their research
- If YOUR website answers their question helpfully, they're more likely to
  call YOU
- Google loves websites that genuinely help people (this is core to E-E-A-T)
- Right now, no one in your local area is doing this well

**Blog post ideas (in order of likely search volume):**

1. **"How Much Does a Burglar Alarm Cost in 2026?"**
   - Cover: basic systems, wireless smart systems, CCTV add-ons, installation
     costs, ongoing costs like app subscriptions and servicing
   - Be honest and transparent - this builds trust
   - Mention your packages as examples with real prices

2. **"Wired vs Wireless Burglar Alarms - Which is Better?"**
   - Explain both options in plain English
   - Give your honest professional opinion
   - Mention that you install wireless systems and why you chose that route

3. **"Will a Burglar Alarm Reduce My Home Insurance?"**
   - Explain how insurance companies view alarms
   - Cover the difference between accredited vs non-accredited installations
   - This is a great reason to get NSI/SSAIB accredited

4. **"Do I Need Planning Permission for CCTV?"**
   - Cover the rules about where you can point cameras
   - GDPR considerations for CCTV
   - This shows expertise and builds trust

5. **"How to Choose a Burglar Alarm Installer (What to Look For)"**
   - Accreditations, insurance, experience, reviews
   - Be honest - even mention what to check (this builds massive trust)
   - Naturally positions MyAlarm as ticking most of these boxes

6. **"Pet-Friendly Burglar Alarms - Everything You Need to Know"**
   - You already mention pet-friendly sensors - expand on this
   - Lots of pet owners search for this specifically

7. **"What to Do If Your Burglar Alarm Keeps Going Off"**
   - Helpful troubleshooting content
   - Positions you as the people to call if they can't fix it
   - Great for bringing in servicing customers

**How to do it:** Write these as blog posts through the CMS. Aim for 500-800
words each. Write as if you're explaining to a friend over a cup of tea - not
formal, not salesy, just helpful. Use photos from real jobs where possible.

### Priority 8: Add FAQs to key pages

**What:** The CMS already supports FAQ sections on every page (this is built
into the template). Adding FAQs helps in two ways:
1. Google often shows FAQ answers directly in search results (more
   visibility)
2. It answers questions that might stop someone from picking up the phone

**Suggested FAQs for the Burglar Alarms page:**
- "How long does it take to install a burglar alarm?"
- "Do you offer a free survey?"
- "Can I keep my pets with a burglar alarm?"
- "What happens if my alarm goes off?"
- "Do you service alarms installed by other companies?"
- "Is there a contract or can I just pay per visit?"

**Suggested FAQs for the CCTV page:**
- "Can I view my CCTV from my phone?"
- "How long is footage stored for?"
- "Do your cameras record in colour at night?"
- "Do I need planning permission for CCTV?"
- "Can I add more cameras later?"

**Suggested FAQs for Location pages:**
- "Do you cover [area name]?"
- "How quickly can you come out to [area name]?"
- "Do you offer free surveys in [area name]?"

**How to do it:** In the CMS, each page has an FAQ section. Add questions and
answers there.

**What:** Make sure your business name, address, and phone number are exactly
the same everywhere online. Google checks this to verify you're a real,
legitimate business.

**Check and update these listings:**
- Google Business Profile (most important)
- Yell.com
- Thomson Local
- Checkatrade (even if not a paid listing)
- Facebook
- Nextdoor
- Bing Places
- Apple Maps

**The details should be exactly:**
- Name: MyAlarm Security
- Address: 40 Northcote Road, Sidcup, Kent, DA14 6PW
- Phone: 020 8302 4065
- Website: https://www.myalarmsecurity.co.uk

**Why it matters:** Google cross-references your details across the internet.
If your name is "MyAlarm Security" on your website but "My Alarm Security"
(with a space) on Yell, Google sees that as inconsistent and trusts you
less.

---

### Priority 10: Add structured data for reviews and services

**What:** This is a small technical change that helps Google understand your
website better. "Structured data" is invisible code that tells Google things
like "this is a review" or "this is a local business" in a way it can easily
read.

**What's needed:**
- LocalBusiness schema (tells Google you're a local business with specific
  opening hours, service areas, etc.)
- Review/AggregateRating schema (tells Google about your 5-star reviews)
- FAQ schema (makes FAQs eligible to appear directly in search results)
- Product schema (for the alarm/CCTV packages with prices)

**Note:** This is a technical change that would need to be done in the website
template code, not through the CMS. The website developer (Chobble) would
need to implement this.
